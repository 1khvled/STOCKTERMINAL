💡 What does DuPont ROE mean in plain English? ROE (Return on Equity) measures how efficiently a company generates profits for its shareholders. The 3-stage DuPont model breaks this down into three core operational drivers:
  • Profitability (Net Profit Margin): How much profit the company makes per $1 of sales. Higher margins mean strong brand value or cost control.
  • Asset Efficiency (Asset Turnover): How productively the company uses its assets to generate sales. Higher turnover means faster inventory or asset utility.
  • Leverage (Equity Multiplier): How much debt the company uses to fund its operations. While debt boosts shareholder returns, high leverage increases solvency risk.

💡 What does the Altman Z-Score mean in plain English? The Altman Z-Score is a trusted quantitative formula used to evaluate a company's financial stability and predict the likelihood of financial distress or bankruptcy within the next 2 years. It compiles 5 crucial financial ratios (measuring liquidity, profitability, productivity, leverage, and activity) into a single score:
🟢 Safe Zone (Z > 2.99) Excellent financial strength. The company is extremely solid with virtually no immediate distress risk.
🟡 Grey Zone (1.81 - 2.99) Moderate risk. The business is stable, but has some leverage or margin pressure. Monitor closely.
🔴 Distress Zone (Z < 1.81) High risk. Indicates serious financial pressure. Warrants deep due diligence on debt obligations.
